CO2 emissions analysis

Comparing countries, regions and emissions type

Last year per capita emissions

Per capita emissions can be misleading indicators, as in our case, especially when countries have very small, wealthy populations that skew the overall assessment.
However, it remains an important metric because it highlights that the wealthiest individuals often pollute significantly more than the average person.
This metric shifts the focus from identifying the most polluting countries to pinpointing the individuals and groups with the highest environmental impact.

Last decade per capita emissions

Again, we see that wealthy countries tend to have higher per capita emissions due to higher living standards, greater consumption of goods and services, energy-intensive industries, and reliance on fossil fuels for energy.
With smaller populations, even high emissions from specific sectors can result in a high per capita average.
