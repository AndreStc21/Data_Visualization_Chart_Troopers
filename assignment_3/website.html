CO2 Emissions Topological Analysis

Comparing the emissions of countries from a topological point of view

2022 - CO2 total emissions map

There are four main polluters: China, the USA, India, and Russia.
These countries have the highest emission levels because they are among the most populous and economically powerful nations in the world.
All other countries contribute significantly less to global pollution compared to these top four emitters.

2022 - CO2 emissions per capita map

This plot shows that the most polluting countries are located in the richer continents.

The countries that stand out the most are the United States of America, Australia and Saudi Arabia.
The U.S.A. has high overall emissions due to its immense wealth, even if it has a large population; while Australia and Saudi Arabia stand out for their high per capita pollution, which is driven by their small population and status of wealthy countries.
