CO2 Emissions Flow Analysis

Comparing the emission flows of countries and their respective regions

In this section, we analyze in detail how CO2 emissions are divided between fossil and land sources, both by country and by region.

Top 3 per region - 2022 CO2 emissions flow

This plot visualizes the flow of CO2 emissions from individual countries (left) to two key sources, fossil fuel and land-use change emissions (middle), and finally to their corresponding regions (right).

- Flows: Represent connections between nodes. The thickness of a flow reflects the magnitude of CO2 emissions contributed by the country to each category and region.

The chart confirms that China, India, and the United States are the top CO2 emitters, with significant contributions from fossil fuels. Russia and Germany follow closely but exhibit relatively less land-use emissions.
In contrast, Brazil and the Democratic Republic of Congo stand out for their high land-use emissions, which are likely tied to deforestation in the Amazon and Congo Basin, respectively.
Interestingly, while wealthier countries dominate in fossil fuel emissions due to industrial activities, land-use emissions showcase the environmental impact in tropical regions, emphasizing the critical role of forest preservation.
